# Sources and confidence

## High-confidence references for the method shape

- Jojo Mayer, `Secret Weapons for the Modern Drummer`, Hudson Music. Publisher
  description lists hand positions, grips, rebound, fulcrum, Gladstone, Moeller
  and finger techniques:
  https://hudsonmusic.com/product/secret-weapons-for-the-modern-drummer/
- Dave Weckl Online School, `Complete Approach to Hand Technique`. Course page
  includes a hand technique exercise path and a `Start Finger Control` lesson:
  https://daveweckl.teachable.com/p/complete-approach-to-hand-technique
- Thomas Lang, Drum Channel `Hand Technique`. Course description emphasizes
  speed, relaxed playing, warm-up, endurance and hand technique:
  https://www.drumchannel.com/thecourses/hand-technique-dca/
- Dom Famularo and Joe Bergamini, `It's Your Move`. Public sample/table of
  contents includes technique foundation and free stroke work:
  https://hudsonmusic.com/wp-content/uploads/Its-Your-Move-SAMPLE.pdf
- Jim Chapin, `Speed, Power, Control, Endurance`, Alfred product page:
  https://www.alfred.com/products/jim-chapin-speed-power-control-endurance-00-32690

## Secondary references

- Modern Drummer profile of Jojo Mayer's `Secret Weapons`, describing it as a

  https://www.moderndrummer.com/2007/11/jojo-mayer-revealing-the-secret/
- MasterClass overview of the Moeller method: speed, control, power and relaxed

  https://www.masterclass.com/articles/the-moeller-method-for-playing-the-drums
- Drumeo/DrumLessons overview of finger control: speed contexts, wrist-to-finger
  transition and doubles:
  https://www.drumlessons.com/drum-lessons/live-lessons-archive/how-to-build-finger-control/

## Confidence labels used in this package

- A: directly supported by named method/course descriptions or standard drum
  technique consensus.
- B: productized extrapolation from common pedagogy, such as exact 30-day
  scheduling and HEYS metrics.
- C: practical heuristic, such as tension thresholds. Useful for safety and UX,
  but should be tuned after user data.

## Attribution rule

Avoid exact claims like "Jojo Mayer considers this the best exercise" unless we
have a direct citation. Safer wording: "this exercise fits the Mayer/Weckl-style
focus on rebound, fulcrum and finger control."
