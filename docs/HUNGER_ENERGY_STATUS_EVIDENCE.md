# Осознанность и баланс энергообмена — Evidence Notes

Evidence appendix for
[HUNGER_ENERGY_STATUS_CONCEPT.md](HUNGER_ENERGY_STATUS_CONCEPT.md). Use this
file to keep studies separate from product logic.

## Current Synthesis

- Intermittent fasting / TRE is useful as an optional behavioral structure, not
  as a guaranteed metabolic advantage.
- Weight-loss results look strongest when the eating window reduces total intake
  or late snacking; when calories are matched, advantages are inconsistent.
- Early TRE may help appetite control, insulin sensitivity, and fat oxidation in
  some settings, but it is not a magic "fat-burning switch".
- Hunger is a mixed signal: meal timing, ghrelin rhythm, sleep, protein/satiety,
  stress, caffeine, and prior meal quality all matter.
- HEYS should use fasting-window context as one input, while Energy Status and
  safety rules keep authority.
- Restriction, poor sleep, stress, and negative affect can raise loss-of-control
  eating risk in susceptible users; this supports a separate Risk Budget layer.
- Low energy availability in active users is a safety/recovery issue, not a
  successful deficit signal.
- Cycle/premenstrual context and appetite/glucose-affecting medication can alter
  hunger; HEYS should treat them as context, not deterministic labels.

## Product Conclusions

- Fasting/TRE should be framed as "meal-gap management", not as a default diet.
- The system should ask: "Is extending this gap safe and useful now?"
- Longer gaps are not automatically better; risk of rebound should be checked
  from current state and personal history.
- A late sweet meal can produce morning hunger without immediate food need.
- Water/tea/black coffee can test thirst/ritual/fatigue hunger, but do not add
  meaningful energy.
- If hunger rises after the checkpoint, the correct move can be a small
  protein/fiber meal, even inside a planned fasting window.

## Claim Matrix

| Claim                                                                       | Evidence level | Product implication                                 | Do not say                   |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------- | --------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Hunger can reflect sleep, stress, circadian rhythm, cues, and meal quality. | moderate       | Show multiple possible drivers and confidence.      | "You are not really hungry." |
